Decisions locked for this round
🔒 Empty / sparse rooms → reassure, don't skip. An empty room is the ideal composite input (clean walls, floor, light). We remove the "skip" path and replace the anxiety with reassurance — protecting the "that's MY room" emotional peak for Jordan & Maya.
🔒 Add a front door (Step 0). A landing/trust screen before upload — hero, 3-second before/after proof, and an "any budget" promise — to clear the trust barrier and Maya's distrust before she commits.
🔒 Mobile-first. Every persona photographs on a phone and shares to TikTok / IG / Close Friends. The result screen in particular is rebuilt phone-first (stacked, not the v0.5 desktop 2-column).
Refined end-to-end flow
v0.5 had 4 steps (Upload → Style/Budget → Analyzing → Result). v0.6 adds a front door and re-sequences Budget before Style. Step 0 is new; all five are mobile-first.

Step 4 · Your moodboard (the emotional peak)
You called this your strongest screen — most of it carries forward. The refinements below close the gap between what's shown (per-item fit) and what the research says each persona actually needs (cohesion, buy-order, a phone-first share).
Add a cohesion signal (≠ per-item match %)
🆕🚨
ChangeAdd an overall "these pieces work as a set" signal at the top of results (a cohesion score / one-line rationale). The v0.5 "98% fit / 94% fit" badges measure each item against the room — not whether the items go together.
WhyMismatch is the #1 pain and the product's core promise (Principle 2). Research literally asks for "cohesion score % for Alex."
Serves🟣◆ Cohesion is the promise
Add buy-priority / spend order
🆕⭐
ChangeRank items with a "buy this first" order + a simple spend breakdown ("start with the rug — it sets the tone"). v0.5 lists products flat with no sequence.
WhyJordan's empty room needs a path, not a pile: "prioritised shopping order" + "buy the top 3 this weekend."
